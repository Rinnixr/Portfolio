Nana Carnival

An immersive VR carnival game created by Nana Studios, a group consisting of Jolie Ng, Evan Talavera, Muhammad Nur Syakir, and myself.

Due to the current global situation, many wide scale events/activities have been put to a halt for roughly 2 years.
This includes leisure entertainment such as concerts, carnivals and conventions.
Our team saw an opportunity in this and wanted to create a VR game that will be able to bring one of such widely missed events back into our daily lives while also remaining responsible.

Design Rationale

With this is mind, we created Nana Carnival, an immersive VR game that brings players into a virtual fairground for them to play in. All mini games within Nana Carnival are similar to real-life carnival mini games, giving players a sense of familiarity.

Nana Carnival was created without an end game in mind. Players can replay all mini games as many times as they want, placing themselves as high as possible on the leaderboard, before deciding to call it a day. This is to provide the same concept as a real-life carnival where you can replay games as many times as you want as long as you have a ticket. Each mini game also has it's own unique leaderboard, meaning players can place differently in each mini game, and that the leaderboard is not an overall score board for the whole game.

For this game, I contributed in the story creation, level design, prop placement, some 3d modeling, animation, UI design and 3D model sourcing online.

Key Features

  • 3D Surround Audio
  • Story Based, Free Exploration
  • Designed with Color-Deficiency & Lower-Limb in Mind
  • Original Assets

Tools & Technologies
